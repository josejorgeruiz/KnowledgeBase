## Mapping Ability, Capability, and Capacity into Spanish Usage

## Habilidad, Capacidad y Alcance  
### A canonical mapping for precise, fair, and human-centered use of the terms ability, capability, and capacity across English and Spanish
## Excerpt
A precise mapping—**Habilidad**, **Capacidad**, **Alcance**—preserves the Canon’s definitions of the **Domains of Competence** while aligning with natural Spanish usage. This paper codifies the mapping and shows how it integrates with the Domains of Competence to keep language clean in practice, not just on paper.

## Abstract
Organizations make costly errors when language blurs distinct constructs. In the Canon, **Ability** denotes present, demonstrable skill; **Capability** denotes the structural power to handle complexity across time; **Capacity** denotes the scope and scale through which ability and capability are applied without losing coherence. Spanish presents overlapping options—*capacidad*, *habilidad*, *aptitud*, *alcance*—that risk collapsing these distinctions. This paper justifies the following mapping: **Ability → Habilidad**, **Capability → Capacidad** (with *capacidad de contribución* as the preferred technical phrase for individual depth), and **Capacity → Alcance** (to mark scope and reach rather than mere throughput). We demonstrate how this lexicon aligns with the **Domains of Competence**, differentiates present execution from judgment-in-time and from span of application, and prevents semantic collisions with adjacent terms such as *Potencial*. The result is a disciplined, field-ready vocabulary that keeps diagnostics, development, operating-model design, and leadership conversations coherent across English–Spanish boundaries.

## Introduction
In cross-language practice, terminological drift corrodes design quality. The Canon’s **Domains of Competence**—**Ability**, **Capability**, **Capacity**—anchor how we assess people, design roles, scale systems, and steward institutions. Spanish, however, concentrates much of this meaning under the seemingly universal *capacidad*. Without a deliberate mapping, leaders will use the right word in English and the wrong one in Spanish, or vice versa, and the system will pay the price in misplacements, brittle promotions, and incoherent scaling.

This concept paper proposes and defends a compact mapping—**Habilidad, Capacidad, Alcance**—that preserves conceptual separation, tracks with everyday Spanish usage, and integrates with the **Domains of Competence**. The aim is not philological purity; it is operational clarity.

## Canonical Distinctions to Preserve

### Ability as Present Skill
**Definition.** Present, demonstrable craft: what a person can reliably do now, observable in execution quality, speed, accuracy, and error rates.  
**Domain of Competence.** Craft discipline; training improves it and performance displays it.

### Capability as Judgment Across Time and Complexity
**Definition.** The structural power to handle layered complexity across horizons—sense-making, meaning-making, and coherent decision-making that sustains intent over time.  
**Domain of Competence.** Judgment discipline; experience, perspective, and cognitive range deepen it.

### Capacity as Scope and Scale of Application
**Definition.** The breadth, reach, and volume through which ability and capability can be applied while preserving alignment and standards—how far choices travel through people, layers, and regions without distortion.  
**Domain of Competence.** Span discipline; organization design and resources expand it.

## The Spanish Landscape and Constraints

### The Field Meaning of “Capacidad”
In Spanish, *capacidad* is elastic. It commonly refers to potential (“*tiene gran capacidad*”), skill (“*capacidad para negociar*”), and bandwidth (“*no tenemos capacidad para más proyectos*”). Its breadth makes it an attractive but dangerous default because it can swallow the other constructs.

### The Field Meaning of “Habilidad”
*Habilidad* lands squarely on concrete, applied competence—“*habilidad técnica*,” “*habilidades interpersonales*.” It points to visible performance that improves with practice and coaching.

### The Field Meaning of “Aptitud”
*Aptitud* leans toward psychometrics and suitability. It is most at home in tests and assessments of innate tilt rather than systemic, layered judgment.

### The Field Meaning of “Alcance”
*Alcance* denotes scope, reach, coverage, and consequences—“*alcance del proyecto*,” “*alcance geográfico*,” “*alcance de esta decisión*.” It describes how far something extends and what it covers.

### Constraints That Govern the Mapping
**Constraint 1: Preserve Canonical Distinctions.** Everyday usage must keep present craft separate from judgment-in-time and from span of application.  
**Constraint 2: Avoid Collision with Potential.** We will need **Potential → Potencial** to name headroom and trajectory; the Spanish lemmas chosen here should not pre-empt that future use.

## Mapping the Domains of Competence into Spanish

### Ability → Habilidad
**Rationale.** *Habilidad* already denotes learned, demonstrable skill. It is visible in performance, trainable through practice and coaching, and measurable by output quality and error rates. The alignment with **Ability** is near one-to-one.

**Canonical Sense in Spanish.** *Habilidad* nombra lo que la persona sabe hacer hoy con destreza y fiabilidad. Su desarrollo ocurre mediante práctica, entrenamiento y retroalimentación directa.

**Examples in Context.**  
“*Su habilidad técnica es sólida, pero aún está desarrollando su capacidad para manejar mayor complejidad.*”  
“*Diseñamos este programa para elevar la habilidad de los mandos medios en conversaciones de desempeño.*”

**Usage Guardrail.** Evitar *capacidad* como sustituto para destrezas prácticas; el uso ambiguo confunde habilidad (ejecución) con capacidad (juicio en complejidad).

### Capability → Capacidad (con “capacidad de contribución”)
**Rationale.** Although *aptitud* was considered, it feels test-driven, risks collision with *Potencial*, and lacks the systemic depth required. In organizational Spanish, *capacidad* comfortably carries leadership, strategic judgment, and depth of work (por ejemplo, *capacidad estratégica*, *capacidad de liderazgo*). To sharpen precision at the individual level, the preferred technical phrase is **capacidad de contribución**—the depth and time-span of coherent judgment a person can sustain. For systems, **capacidad organizativa/institucional** carries the same construct to higher layers.

**Canonical Sense in Spanish.** *Capacidad (de contribución)* nombra el poder de sostener complejidad y horizonte temporal con buen juicio, sentido y coherencia.

**Examples in Context.**  
“*Su capacidad de contribución ya está por encima del nivel del rol actual.*”  
“*La capacidad organizativa para manejar esta complejidad regulatoria todavía es limitada.*”

**Usage Guardrail.** Reservar *aptitud* para contextos psicométricos, no para juicios sobre complejidad y horizonte temporal.

### Capacity → Alcance
**Rationale.** We intentionally reframed **Capacity** in English to mean the **span of application**—how far ability and capability propagate through people, interfaces, and regions while preserving coherence—not merely throughput. Spanish has a precise word for span: **alcance**. Choosing *alcance* prevents *capacidad* from doing triple duty and keeps *capacidad* focused on depth of judgment.

**Canonical Sense in Spanish.** *Alcance* es la cobertura real de la acción y del estándar a través de capas, equipos y territorios. Para throughput, utilícense calificadores explícitos como *capacidad operativa* o *capacidad instalada*; para la idea canónica de **Capacity**, prefíerese *alcance*.

**Examples in Context.**  
“*El fundador tiene la capacidad de diseñar una buena estrategia, pero su alcance hoy es una oficina con 20 personas.*”  
“*El reto del nuevo CEO no es sólo aumentar la capacidad, sino extender el alcance sin perder coherencia.*”

**Usage Guardrail.** Emplear *alcance* cuando se trata de cobertura, propagación o radio de acción; mantener *capacidad* para juicio y complejidad.

## Alignment with the Domains of Competence

### Habilidad (Ability)
**Dominio.** Ejecución presente con destreza y fiabilidad.  
**Signals.** Calidad, velocidad, precisión y tasa de error evidencian el nivel.  
**Levers.** Entrenamiento, práctica deliberada, estándares observables y feedback inmediato mueven la aguja.

### Capacidad (Capability)
**Dominio.** Manejo de complejidad y horizonte temporal mediante juicio, sentido y coherencia.  
**Signals.** Calidad de decisiones sin feedback inmediato, consistencia de criterio en horizontes largos, habilidad para sostener ambigüedad.  
**Levers.** Ampliación de horizontes, marcos de decisión, exposición a problemas no lineales y responsabilidades reales con patrocinio.

### Alcance (Capacity)
**Dominio.** Extensión de la aplicación de habilidad y capacidad a través de personas y unidades sin degradar estándares.  
**Signals.** Consistencia interregional, latencia de decisión a ejecución, tasa de retrabajo en cascada, fidelidad de estándares a distancia.  
**Levers.** Diseño organizativo, interfaces claras, gobernanza y guardrails, plataformas y recursos adecuados.

**Canonical Sentence.** “*Una líder puede tener alta capacidad de contribución y buena habilidad operativa, pero si el diseño organizativo limita su alcance, la estrategia no va a sentirse igual en todas las regiones.*”

## Practical Guidance for Consistent Usage

### Choosing the Right Term
**When observing present performance,** prefer *habilidad*. Preguntas operativas del tipo “¿puede ejecutar esta tarea con calidad y ritmo?” pertenecen aquí.  
**When estimating judgment across complexity and time,** prefer *capacidad (de contribución)*. Indaga “¿hasta qué nivel de complejidad y qué horizonte temporal sostiene su criterio?”  
**When asking about reach or coverage,** prefer *alcance*. Consulta “¿hasta dónde viaja la intención y el estándar sin distorsión?”

### Durable Phrasing Patterns
**Habilidad para tareas concretas.** Formular “habilidad para [tarea]” cuando el foco sea destreza observable.  
**Capacidad de contribución por horizonte.** Formular “capacidad de contribución para [complejidad/horizonte]” cuando el foco sea juicio sostenido.  
**Alcance de estrategia, liderazgo u operación.** Formular “alcance de [estrategia/liderazgo/operación] a través de [equipos/regiones]” cuando el foco sea propagación del estándar.

### Guardrails That Prevent Collisions
**Avoiding the “capacidad-absoluta”.** No llame *capacidad* a todo. Si habla de throughput, diga *capacidad operativa*; si habla de span, diga *alcance*.  
**Separating “potencial”.** *Potencial* designa holgura de crecimiento y trayectoria futura; no lo desplace usando *aptitud* o *capacidad* en contextos de liderazgo.  
**Naming throughput versus span.** Cuando el objetivo es volumen, nombre volumen; cuando el objetivo es cobertura, nombre alcance.

## Edge Cases and How to Write Them

### Throughput Versus Span
**Ambiguo.** “Queremos aumentar la capacidad del área.”  
**Claro (throughput).** “Queremos aumentar la capacidad operativa del área en 20%.”  
**Claro (span).** “Queremos ampliar el alcance de la operación a dos regiones adicionales sin degradar estándares.”

### Skill Versus Judgment
**Confuso.** “Le falta capacidad en análisis de datos.”  
**Preciso (skill).** “Le falta habilidad en análisis de datos: funciones avanzadas y modelado.”  
**Preciso (judgment).** “Su capacidad de contribución para decisiones interfuncionales aún es incipiente.”

### Span Versus Authority
**Confuso.** “Su alcance incluye aprobar todas las inversiones.”  
**Preciso.** “Su alcance abarca estandarizar criterios y asegurar su adopción; las aprobaciones mayores escalan al comité.”

## Worked Examples Across Scales

### Founder Stage
Fuerte **capacidad** para diseñar y pivotear con horizontes cortos a medios; **alcance** limitado a una sede y 20 personas; **habilidades** comerciales en desarrollo. El primer diseño debe extender el **alcance** instalando interfaces y guardrails antes de sumar headcount.

### Fortune 100 CEO
Alta **capacidad (de contribución)** para sostener complejidad multi-año; **alcance** global a través de múltiples regiones; **habilidad** comunicativa que convierte dirección en estándares operables. El riesgo estratégico es sobrecargar la **capacidad**; protegerla con horizontes claros y derechos de decisión, y ampliar **alcance** mediante plataformas.

### Functional Leader
Sólida **habilidad** técnica; **capacidad** emergente para decisiones de 12–18 meses; **alcance** actual de tres equipos en una región. Un plan de desarrollo efectivo aumenta **alcance** por etapas mientras alarga el horizonte de decisiones con andamiaje.

## Integration with Adjacent Canon

### Levels of Work and Contribution Bands
Use **capacidad de contribución** to calibrate horizon and complexity a role holder can sustain; use **alcance** to specify the radius through which that contribution must travel without distortion.

### DOES Cycle (Design–Organize–Execute–Sustain)
**Capacidad** concentrates in **Design** and **Sustain** (juicio y renovación), **Habilidad** se expresa en **Execute**, y **Alcance** se ingenieriza en **Organize** mediante interfaces, roles y guardrails.

### Triad of Direction (Management–Leadership–Stewardship)
The canon definition of **Domains of Competence** names **what** we measure—craft, judgment-in-time, and span—across the modes of direction. Keep these domains conceptually distinct from the **Triad of Direction** to avoid semantic overload.

## Conclusion
Precision in vocabulary is not pedantry; it is design integrity. Mapping **Ability → Habilidad**, **Capability → Capacidad** (as **capacidad de contribución** where precision is required), and **Capacity → Alcance** keeps the Canon’s architecture intact in Spanish. It honors natural usage, prevents collisions with *Potencial*, and gives practitioners handles that work in meetings, operating-model diagrams, talent reviews, and board briefings alike.

Used as the Canon’s **Domains of Competence**, these constructs discipline how we observe, develop, and scale: **Habilidad** names present craft, **Capacidad** names judgment across complexity and time, and **Alcance** names span of application. Scaling then reads as a coherent sentence in both languages: **extend alcance without outstripping capacidad or burning through habilidad**.

*Keywords:* ability, capability, capacity, habilidad, capacidad, alcance, domains of competence, Spanish terminology, organizational design, leadership semantics

## Mapping Assessment, Evaluation, and Appreciation into Spanish Usage


By Jose J. Ruiz

## Valoración, Evaluación y Apreciación  
### A canonical mapping for precise, fair, and human-centered use of the terms assessment, evaluation, and appreciation across English and Spanish

## Excerpt
This concept paper distinguishes *assessment*, *evaluation*, and *appreciation* as three different leadership moves—information-gathering, judgment-assigning, and interpretive stewardship—and provides a clean Spanish mapping (*valoración, evaluación y apreciación*) that preserves their boundaries. The result is a teachable architecture for diagnosis without verdicts, verdicts with standards, and longitudinal human judgment that guides growth.

## Abstract
Language shapes practice. In talent decisions, the habitual blending of *assessment*, *evaluation*, and *appreciation* produces avoidable noise, weakens trust, and distorts outcomes. This paper clarifies each term, shows how they relate in English, and then codifies a Spanish mapping that preserves the distinctions: **Assessment → Valoración**, **Evaluation → Evaluación**, **Appreciation → Apreciación**. We define purposes, outputs, and constraints for each discipline and propose a simple operating sequence—**Assess → Evaluate → Appreciate**—that moves from disciplined diagnosis to accountable verdicts to developmental interpretation. The mapping aligns with leadership and governance practice: *assessment* supplies structured information without judgments; *evaluation* applies standards and carries consequences; *appreciation* integrates time, complexity, and dignity to shape pacing and growth. With precise terms, organizations can teach the architecture of talent decisions, reduce bias from category errors, and make decisions that are fair, auditable, and humane across languages.

## Introduction
Every serious talent system must answer three different questions: *What do we know?* *What must we decide?* *How do we steward growth over time?* In English these questions align to three disciplines—assessment, evaluation, and appreciation. In practice, they are often conflated. Assessments become verdicts by stealth. Evaluations are softened into praise that clouds consequence. Appreciation is mistaken for scoring or calibration, stripping it of its developmental value. This confusion is magnified in bilingual environments where English and Spanish are used interchangeably; Spanish frequently deploys “evaluación” as an umbrella term, dissolving a fine-grained architecture into a single, blunt move. Precision matters because decisions carry consequence, and consequence demands conceptual discipline.

This paper restores the boundaries. First, it clarifies how the three terms work in English. Second, it explains why a one-to-one translation into Spanish fails and proposes a cleaner alternative. Third, it specifies definitions and operating constraints so leaders can teach, audit, and improve practice across cultures.

## The Canon in English

### Assessment: Structured discovery without verdict
Assessment is an **information-gathering** discipline. It is a structured process to collect data about behavior, patterns, capability signals, skills, and potential. The constraint is decisive: *no value judgment yet*. No pass/fail, no rankings, no comparison to standards. Properly used, assessment produces **inputs**—evidence that informs decisions, development plans, role matching, and risk management. Conceptually, it belongs to diagnostic work and sense-making: the careful act of seeing what is present before deciding what it means or what to do.

### Evaluation: Standards, criteria, and consequence
Evaluation is a **judgment-assigning** discipline. It applies explicit standards, criteria, or thresholds to decide *how good, how fit, or how ready* someone or something is. Evaluation includes comparison to norms, ratings, pass/fail gates, promotion decisions, calibration, and pay outcomes. It converts evidence into **verdicts** with articulated consequences. Where assessment asks, “What is true about the evidence?”, evaluation asks, “Given our standards, what must we decide?”

### Appreciation: Longitudinal, human-centered judgment
Appreciation is **interpretive judgment over time**. It integrates how a person handles complexity, context, and extended time horizons and attends to dignity, potential, and growth. It is judgment, but not a narrow score. Appreciation draws on evidence from assessments and the outcomes of evaluations, yet it transcends both by reading trajectory and shaping pacing. Its output is not a rating but a **developmental hypothesis** that informs role shaping, stretch sequencing, and stewardship of the person-in-context.

### The English Stack: Diagnosis, Decision, Development
When taught cleanly, the stack is straightforward: **Assessment** gathers information without verdicts. **Evaluation** applies standards and issues a verdict. **Appreciation** interprets the person’s developmental arc and the fit between growth and context. Diagnosis → Decision → Development.

## Why Spanish Precision Matters
A literal translation that renders *assessment* as **evaluación** collapses the very constraint that defines it: *no judgment yet*. This produces two failures. First, it blurs the boundary between evidence and verdict, making it harder to defend decisions ethically and legally. Second, it erases the longitudinal, human-centered work of appreciation by folding everything into a single evaluative act. Because Spanish usage often treats “evaluación” as a broad umbrella, the architecture disappears just where leaders most need clarity—around promotions, pay, succession, remediation, and developmental pacing. If the canon is to travel across languages without distortion, the distinctions must be preserved.

## A Canonical Spanish Mapping

### Valoración = Assessment
**Valoración** (or *proceso de valoración*) emphasizes observation and diagnosis without implying pass/fail. It is widely used in clinical and organizational contexts as a softer, more diagnostic term. As *assessment*, valoración collects and organizes information so later decisions can be informed. Its defining constraint—no verdict—is kept intact.

### Evaluación = Evaluation
**Evaluación** remains the verdict-oriented term. It applies criteria and thresholds, compares performance to expectations or norms, and produces consequences: selection, promotion, compensation, deployment. It names the accountable decision step that interprets evidence against standards in a specified timeframe.

### Apreciación = Appreciation
**Apreciación** already serves as an elegant translation. It denotes qualitative, interpretive judgment over time. Apreciación integrates evidence from valoración and outcomes from evaluación, but its purpose is distinct: to read capability-in-time, estimate transition points, and shape humane, sustainable growth.

## Operating Sequence and Guardrails

### Step One: Valoración (Assessment)
Conduct structured discovery. Use validated instruments, behavioral interviews, work samples, and decision reviews to produce a clear picture of present repertoire and elicitable patterns. Label artifacts “diagnóstico—sin veredicto.” Document limitations and intended uses. The output is evidence, not a decision.

### Step Two: Evaluación (Evaluation)
Apply standards to the evidence. Make the criteria explicit, publish weightings, and record trade-offs. State consequences and revisit dates. The output is a verdict tied to standards within a declared horizon—hire/no-hire, promote/hold, increase/maintain, deploy/redeploy.

### Step Three: Apreciación (Appreciation)
Offer a developmental interpretation that honors dignity and time. Estimate current and emerging capability, anticipate transitions, and propose pacing and scaffolding. Keep appreciation out of pay and discipline channels; its power lies in guiding growth and preventing brittle moves.

### Guardrails for Auditability
Guardrails keep the three from bleeding into one another. Assessments must not be used to rank or gate; evaluations must not pretend to be neutral; appreciations must not become backdoor ratings. Verbs help: **valorar** to describe, **evaluar** to decide, **apreciar** to develop.

## Implications for Governance and Fairness
Clear boundaries do more than tidy language; they strengthen fairness. When valoración is kept diagnostic, organizations can apply quality controls—construct clarity, reliability, validity, bias audits—without the pressure of immediate consequence. When evaluación is explicitly standard-based, decision makers can publish rationales, horizons, and review triggers, enabling contestability and calibration. When apreciación is protected from pay and discipline channels, it can honor context and potential, informing stretch and support without weaponizing development. This separation also clarifies ownership: accredited practitioners or People Analytics teams own valoración protocols; accountable leaders or committees own evaluative verdicts; coaches, mentors, or capability councils own apreciación and pacing.

## Role Fit, Capability, and Time
The trio clarifies the relationship between current skill, judgment in complexity, and sustainable load. Valoración can describe **ability** (present-tense skill and know-how) and surface signals that suggest **capability** (judgment across longer horizons). Evaluación decides **fit** relative to standards and risk posture for a specific role and timeframe. Apreciación reads **trajectory**, estimating when expanded scope becomes sustainable and when consolidation is prudent. Conflating these leads to brittle promotions (ability mistaken for capability), unfair remediation (verdicts rendered on diagnostic noise), and wasted development (praise without pacing).

## Teaching the Architecture

### Name the Step You Are In
Open conversations explicitly: “Estamos en valoración” or “Entramos a evaluación.” Naming the step reminds participants of constraints and outputs.

### Label the Artifacts
Mark assessment reports as “Diagnóstico—sin veredicto,” evaluation briefs as “Veredicto y criterio aplicado,” and appreciation profiles as “Hipótesis de desarrollo—sin implicaciones de compensación.” Labels prevent leakage across channels.

### Sequence the Reviews
Schedule valoración reviews before evaluative calibrations; reserve apreciación for post-decision pacing and role shaping. The order matters: evidence first, verdict second, stewardship last.

### Audit Language in Systems
Replace a single umbrella “evaluación” in forms and platforms with distinct modules: *valoración* (diagnostic), *evaluación* (verdict), *apreciación* (developmental). Terminology drives behavior.

## Formal Definitions

### Valoración (Assessment)
**Definición.** *Valoración* es un proceso estructurado para recopilar y organizar información sobre la personalidad, el comportamiento, los patrones de pensamiento, las habilidades y las señales de potencial de una persona **sin asignar valor, realizar comparaciones ni determinar resultados**. Su propósito es construir una base de evidencia para la toma de decisiones, el desarrollo y la adecuación rol–persona. El resultado es un retrato descriptivo con límites y errores declarados, etiquetado explícitamente como diagnóstico y no decisional.

### Evaluación (Evaluation)
**Definición.** *Evaluación* es la aplicación de criterios, estándares o umbrales para emitir un **juicio explícito** sobre el desempeño, la idoneidad o la preparación de una persona en relación con un rol, nivel o resultado esperado. Incluye comparaciones, calificaciones, decisiones de pasar/no pasar y consecuencias (por ejemplo, selección, promoción, compensación, despliegue). El resultado es un veredicto con horizonte y rationale documentados.

### Apreciación (Appreciation)
**Definición.** *Apreciación* es un juicio cualitativo e interpretativo que comprende y valora cómo una persona hace sentido de la complejidad a lo largo de horizontes de tiempo más largos. Integra evidencia de valoración y resultados de evaluación, pero los trasciende al considerar contexto, trayectoria, dignidad y potencial en el tiempo. El resultado es una hipótesis de desarrollo y una propuesta de ritmo y apoyos, sin puntajes ni implicaciones de compensación.

## Practical Applications

### Hiring
Valoración produce evidencia estructurada alineada a los requisitos del rol; evaluación emite la decisión de contratación con intercambios documentados; apreciación configura onboarding y primeros *stretches* para evitar sobrecarga.

### Promotion and Succession
Valoración actualiza la base de evidencia; evaluación establece veredictos de preparación con disparadores de revisión; apreciación secuencia aumentos de complejidad y soportes para consolidar el crecimiento.

### Performance Remediation
Valoración aísla brechas de habilidad o restricciones sistémicas; evaluación decide entre rediseño, apoyo, redistribución o salida; apreciación protege la dignidad e identifica una ruta sostenible aun cuando las consecuencias sean firmes.

## Conclusion
When leaders conflate diagnosis, verdict, and development, talent systems become noisy and fragile. When they separate the disciplines—and teach the Spanish mapping **valoración, evaluación y apreciación**—practice becomes clearer, fairer, and more humane. *Valoración* gathers evidence without verdicts. *Evaluación* applies standards and takes responsibility for consequences. *Apreciación* interprets the person-in-context across time and designs growth that honors dignity. The architecture is simple enough to teach, strong enough to audit, and humane enough to trust.

Keywords: assessment, evaluation, appreciation, valoración, evaluación, apreciación, talent decisions, capability, development, governance

## Mapping Tending and Stewardship into Spanish Usage


By Jose J. Ruiz

**Cuidado** and **Custodia** Integrated with the Domains of Competence

---
## Excerpt

This concept paper fixes **Custodia** as the canonical Spanish lemma for *Stewardship* and **Cuidado** as the best lemma for *Tending*. It shows how **Cuidado** and **Custodia** function as “two kinds of care” across different planes—local practice and enterprise guardianship—and how they integrate with the **Trípode del Trabajo** (Tarea, Confianza, Cuidado), the **Tríada de Dirección** (Gestión, Liderazgo, Custodia), and the **Dominios de Competencia** (**Habilidad**, **Capacidad**, **Alcance**).

## Abstract

The canon distinguishes two care disciplines that must remain conceptually separate to keep organizations both humane and durable. *Tending* is the lived, local discipline that maintains people, interfaces, and learning in the flow of work. *Stewardship* is the enterprise guardianship that protects identity, ethics, and the license to operate across long horizons. With **Custodia** locked for stewardship, the Spanish lemma for tending must feel like everyday, local care and pair cleanly with **Custodia** as “dos formas de cuidado.” This paper establishes **Cuidado** for tending; reaffirms the Spanish mapping for the Domains of Competence—**Habilidad** (Ability), **Capacidad** (Capability, with *capacidad de contribución* as the technical phrase), and **Alcance** (Capacity as application span); and integrates these with the **Trípode del Trabajo** and the **Tríada de Dirección** (Gestión, Liderazgo, Custodia). Definitions, contrast lines, and canonical examples are provided to stabilize usage in role design, governance, and daily operations.

## Introduction

Translation in organizational design is architecture by other means. Terms carry distinctions that steer decisions, shape interfaces, and determine how intent travels at scale. The system requires two planes of care. At the node, teams need a discipline that keeps work humane and truly practiced—micro-repair, relational hygiene, and learning rhythms that actually occur. At the enterprise, leaders need a guardianship that preserves identity, ethics, and legitimacy across horizons—covenants, guardrails, and incentive structures that outlast any single tenure. With **Custodia** set for stewardship, the open design question is which Spanish lemma best names tending without blurring frames. The answer we advance is **Cuidado**.

## Fixing *Stewardship* as **Custodia**

Stewardship is enterprise guardianship. Its object is identity, ethics, and the license to operate; its scope is the enterprise and ecosystem, including boards and senior stewards; its horizon is measured in years and decades; its mode is macro-repair via covenants, guardrails, and incentive architecture. **Custodia** names this field in Spanish with precision: it signals responsible custody without implying ownership, and it naturally collocates with governance and institutional continuity. Within the **Tríada de Dirección**, **Custodia** stands alongside **Gestión** (reliability in the known) and **Liderazgo** (direction amid uncertainty) to ensure what performs today also endures tomorrow.

## Selecting *Tending* as **Cuidado**

### Framing with Custodia Fixed

Given **Custodia** for stewardship, the term for tending must denote concrete, day-to-day care; remain firmly inside the practice frame (not the legal or institutional frame); sit cleanly inside the **Trípode del Trabajo** beside Tarea and Confianza; and avoid collisions with DOES (especially Sustain/Sostener) and governance language.

### Rationale for **Cuidado**

**Cuidado** is idiomatic for lived, local care in organizational Spanish. Teams naturally “cuidan” relaciones, procesos, límites e interfaces; facilitators “cuidan” ritmos y aprendizaje; and leaders “cuidan” el espacio para conversaciones difíciles. The term covers micro-repair and human rhythm without drifting into legalism. Most importantly, **Cuidado** pairs elegantly with **Custodia** to express two planes of care: *El Cuidado es la disciplina local; la Custodia es la guardianía institucional*. This pairing is memorable, teachable, and operationally useful.

### Why Alternatives Fall Short

**Atención** centers noticing and responsiveness but weakly denotes repair and relational upkeep; as a pillar name it risks shrinking the discipline to mere issue response. **Acompañamiento** evokes a facilitation posture more than an operating discipline of a node; it reads closer to coaching style than to system practice. **Contención** is crisis-weighted and too narrow for everyday health. **Cultivo** is a strong metaphor for growth and development but over-indexes on development rather than repair and rhythm; it also risks confusion with capability-building constructs. **Sostener/Sostenimiento** collides directly with DOES (“Sustain”), blurring distinct system layers. In contrast, **Cuidado** is plain, local, and precise once framed in the canon.

## The Trípode del Trabajo in Spanish

The **Trípode del Trabajo** encodes the disciplines that make autonomy safe and productive inside a bounded node.

**Tarea** clarifies mandates and constraints so action is possible without guesswork. It defines what must be done, why it matters, and the guardrails within which discretion is granted. The act is **Encomendar**.

**Confianza** designs decision rights and verification so discretion is both granted and auditable. Trust is not a hope but a design choice realized through clear interfaces, escalation paths, and review rhythms. **The act is Confiar**.

**Cuidado** keeps people, interfaces, and learning healthy. It performs micro-repair, maintains human rhythm, and ensures the node’s routines—retros, reviews, renewals—actually occur. In short: **Cuidado** makes autonomy humane in real time. The act is **Cuidar**.

Read together and aloud—*Trípode del Trabajo: Encomendar, Confiar y Cuidar*—the triad is legible and easy to teach. Capitalization signals that **Cuidado** is a named discipline, not a sentiment. **Cuidar** implies care, not supervision. 

## The Tríada de Dirección in Spanish

The **Tríada de Dirección** composes and safeguards enterprise direction across nodes and time.

**Gestión** secures reliability in the known. It builds standards, cadences, and feedback loops so systems deliver consistently without micromanagement. Its horizon is months to a year; its virtue is dependable throughput within clear constraints.

**Liderazgo** composes direction amid uncertainty. It frames the problem space, selects consequential bets, communicates intent, and adapts as contexts shift. Its horizon traverses quarters to several years; its virtue is judgment under ambiguity.

**Custodia** preserves identity and coherence across horizons. It binds means to ends, installs guardrails, and designs incentives that prevent value drift. Its horizon is multi-year to generational; its virtue is continuity with integrity. Together, Gestión, Liderazgo y Custodia prevent stagnation, drift, and erosion of principle, respectively.

## The Domains of Competence in Spanish

A shared diagnostic grammar prevents teams from talking past each other. The canon’s triad cleanly separates present skill, structural judgment, and application span, and in Spanish maps as follows.

**Habilidad (Ability).** Present, demonstrable skill and know-how. It is visible in execution today and improves with practice, coaching, and feedback. Diagnostics center on quality, speed, accuracy, stability, and safety.

**Capacidad (Capability).** Structural power to handle complexity across time while maintaining coherence. It is the depth of judgment and sense-making a person can sustain as span and horizon extend. When precision is needed, use **capacidad de contribución** to name the level and time span at which a person or system can add value reliably.

**Alcance (Capacity).** The application span of ability and capability: how far, how wide, and through how many teams, layers, and regions intent and standards can travel without distortion. This deliberately reframes capacity away from mere throughput and toward **reach with integrity**, enabling phrases like *alcance operativo* or *alcance de la estrategia*.

The three domains interact multiplicatively. **Habilidad** bounds execution quality; **Capacidad** bounds altitude and time depth; **Alcance** bounds how far judgment and craft can travel intact. Lifting only one shifts the constraint elsewhere.

## How **Cuidado** and **Custodia** Interlock with **Habilidad, Capacidad, Alcance**

**Cuidado** is enacted through **Habilidad**—the micro-skills of difficult conversations, interface hygiene, facilitation, and learning design. It presupposes **Capacidad** to read context and time interventions; without it, care becomes misplaced effort. Healthy **Cuidado** expands **Alcance** because standards and intent transmit with less friction when interfaces are tended and rhythms are kept.

**Custodia** relies on **Capacidad** at institutional scale—meaning-making across long horizons and the translation of purpose into guardrails, commons, and succession. Its contribution to **Alcance** is indirect but decisive: when guardrails are explicit and culture codifies non-negotiables, thousands of peripheral decisions align without central adjudication. **Custodia** also requires specific **Habilidades**—crafting mandates and red lines, diagnosing irreversibility, architecting incentives that reward honorable means, not only ends.

The feedback loop is simple and powerful: **Cuidado** surfaces ground truth; **Custodia** reforms the system in response. Two planes, one logic of care.

## Canon-Faithful Sentences and Examples (Spanish)

**Contrast of planes.**  
El **Cuidado** es la disciplina local y vivida; la **Custodia** es la guardianía institucional de largo horizonte.

**Micro vs. macro repair.**  
El **Cuidado** hace la microreparación; la **Custodia** hace la macroreparación.

**Transmission of intent.**  
Sin **Cuidado** de interfaces, el **alcance** de la estrategia se degrada; con **Custodia** clara, la intención viaja intacta a los bordes.

**Role calibration.**  
Su **habilidad** operativa es sólida; su **capacidad de contribución** permite sostener decisiones a 24 meses; su **alcance** actual cubre dos plantas sin pérdida de estándares.

**Triad interplay.**  
La **Gestión** instala el ciclo común; el **Liderazgo** compone tres apuestas; la **Custodia** define criterios de irreversibilidad y sucesión.

## Implementation Notes for Designers and Coaches

Anchor diagnoses in the domains. If execution falters, name **Habilidad** and specify the skills to build. If judgment collapses with longer horizons or wider span, name **Capacidad** and recalibrate contribution level, coaching, or structure. If intent dies at the edges, name **Alcance** and redesign interfaces, standards, and replication mechanisms.

Treat **Cuidado** as a named discipline in artifacts and diagrams. Capitalize it; bind it to its object set—personas, interfaces, aprendizaje—and locate it explicitly in the **Trípode del Trabajo**. Use ordinary Spanish verbs to keep it lived: *cuidar el límite*, *cuidar el ritmo del equipo*, *cuidar la relación con upstream y downstream*. Reserve **Custodia** for the long-arc work in the **Tríada de Dirección**; when emphasis is needed, *Custodia institucional* disambiguates the frame.

Prevent collisions by design. Avoid *Sostener* as a pillar label (it belongs to DOES). Keep **Capacidad** distinct from *potencial* to separate structural contribution from headroom for growth. Maintain **Alcance** as application span, not throughput, so scaling remains the art of making intent and standards travel intact.

## Conclusion

With **Custodia** fixed for *Stewardship*, selecting **Cuidado** for *Tending* yields a crisp, teachable grammar: **dos formas de cuidado** en dos planos distintos. **Cuidado** keeps the node humane and healthy in real time; **Custodia** preserves identity, ethics, and license to operate across horizons. These sit alongside **Gestión** and **Liderazgo** in the **Tríada de Dirección**, and atop the **Dominios de Competencia**—**Habilidad**, **Capacidad** (*capacidad de contribución*), and **Alcance**—which provide the diagnostic backbone for design and governance. Adopted together, this Spanish preserves conceptual boundaries, prevents system collisions, and enables responsible scaling: extend **Alcance** sin desbordar **Capacidad** ni agotar **Habilidad**, practicando **Cuidado** localmente y ejerciendo **Custodia** institucionalmente.

## Use of Domains of Competence Words

## Instruction

Treat the **Glossary** as the authoritative source. The Domains of Competence terms **Ability**, **Capability**, and **Capacity** are **reserved words** and must only be used with the Glossary meanings below.


## Do-not-change exemplars (canonical)

Do not modify these examples; they demonstrate correct application and expected rewrite patterns.

- “She has the ability to influence senior stakeholders.” → “She influences senior stakeholders.”
- “He has the capability to manage global programs.” → “He manages global programs and orchestrates vendors.”
- “They have the capacity to take more accounts.” → “They have bandwidth to take five more enterprise accounts.”

---

## How to change when usage is incorrect

### Preferred: active-verb rewrites (default)
Remove the misused reserved term and express the claim as observable action, outcome, or operating behavior.

### Allowed: term swap (only when clearly correct)
Swap Ability/Capability/Capacity only when the sentence’s intended meaning is unambiguous and matches the Glossary definition of the replacement term.

---

## Quick rewrite templates

- **Ability misused for complexity across time horizons** → replace with **Capability** or rewrite actively.
  - “has the ability to set multi-year direction” → “has the capability to set multi-year direction”
  - or → “sets direction across multi-year horizons.”

- **Capability misused for present-tense skill** → replace with **Ability** or active verb.
  - “has the capability to code in Python” → “has the ability to code in Python”
  - or → “codes in Python.”

- **Capacity misused for skill or judgment** → use scope/scale/bandwidth language (and avoid implying “skill”).
  - “has the capacity to learn quickly” → “shows aptitude for rapid learning.”
  - “has the capacity for five more clients” → “has bandwidth for five more clients.”

---

## Alternative wording menu (use when changing)

- **Demonstrated skill (now):** proficient at, skilled at, fluent in, adept at, delivers, executes, produces
- **Readiness & fit:** equipped to, positioned to, well-suited to, ready to
- **Potential & trajectory:** shows promise in, has aptitude for, emerging strength in, on track to
- **Scope & complexity (instead of misusing “capability”):** navigates, orchestrates, sustains, integrates, governs, frames trade-offs
- **Throughput/scale (instead of misusing “capacity”):** bandwidth for, headroom for, room to take on, coverage for, can absorb

---

## Operational checks

- **Ambiguity rule:** if context is ambiguous but plausibly aligns with the Glossary meaning, **do not swap terms**. You may optionally rewrite for clarity without changing intent.
- **Semantics guardrail:** preserve original intent, scope, subject, tense, and polarity when changing.
- **No inflation:** do not add strength modifiers (e.g., “world-class”) unless present in the source.
- **Consistency:** apply the same decision rule across comparable sentences within the same document.

---

## Fast tests to choose the right concept

- Mentions judgment under uncertainty, meaning-making, coherence across layers, or longer time horizons → **Capability**.
- Describes hands-on execution that can be reliably observed now → **Ability**.
- Specifies “how much / how many / how broad,” scale/reach, or bandwidth **while preserving coherence** → **Capacity** (or “bandwidth/headroom/coverage”).

## Instruction

## Common misuses and how to fix

### Misuse: “Leadership” used for routine control/execution
- “Shows leadership in enforcing SOP checklists”  
  → “Manages SOP compliance and daily adherence.”  
  *(or)* → “Ensures SOP compliance and daily adherence.”

### Misuse: “Management” used for direction under uncertainty
- “Manages a multi-year strategy shift”  
  → “Leads a multi-year strategy shift amid market change.”  
  *(or)* → “Sets direction for a multi-year strategy shift amid market change.”

### Misuse: “Stewardship” used for short-term performance tasks
- “Owns dashboard stewardship”  
  → “Manages the performance dashboard.”  
  *(or, if the intent is truly long-horizon governance)* → “Stewards data ethics and long-term governance.”

### Misuse: “Leadership” used when the emphasis is legacy/ethics/identity
- “Leads our code-of-conduct program”  
  → “Stewards our ethics and conduct standards over time.”  
  *(If the sentence is primarily operational administration, prefer a Management rewrite instead.)*
